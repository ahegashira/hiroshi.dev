About

Up until 2016 or so, I spent my time pursuing a sales career. I enjoyed the the people and the process and felt that I would stay on that path for the foreseeable future. During that time, what I was ignoring was what I really wanted to do. I spent most of my time devoted to the growth of my sales numbers and pushing my product but when I took a step back I realized that my personal growth was slowing down. I had caught up to many of the people who taught me in many ways but had a desire to do more.

I moved around a little bit and explored other areas of the industry to try and spark that interest again but it was gone and I needed to make a change. After some time to reflect on where I was and where I wanted to be, I realized that I had been spending my spare time both at work and at home creating algorithms and basic scripts in excel to make my job easier. My new path became clear at this point; I was going to finally learn to program.

My initial estimate was that my family could go for up to 6 months without my income before we would get nervous and I would have to go back to where I came from. I signed up on Treehouse and started their Frontend program. Ultimately I knew that the backend is probably where I would take the strongest interest but my thought at the time was that the front-end was easier to pick up and that I could put together some websites while looking for steady work.

Four months later, I had devoted 6-8 hours a day, 5-6 days a week to learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. This was enough to prove to myself that this wasn't just a phase. I had devoted a solid block of time to learning new skills and not only followed a self-paced program but also structured my own supplemental curriculum and I loved every minute of it.

Despite the initial success I have had I still felt that something was missing. Getting into tech isn't a solo venture in most cases and I was learning how to do everything as if it was. I needed to work with others to solve problems and implement the solutions. This brings me to where I am now in my development as a full time programming student focused on database and software development. By 2020 I believe that I will have a well rounded background in both frontend and backend languages to be valuable to any company.

Skills

Since skill level is such a difficult and subjective measurement I'll be using three levels of ability.

  • The lowest level only indicating that I have exposed myself to the language. By this I mean that I have done some exercises and can recognize the language when it it put in front of me. I can probably write some basic functions but this has not been my focus.
  • The middle level will represent languages which I have taken more interest in and have devoted more time to. I am in no way an expert in these but feel competent enough that given some time I could work out a problem.
  • The highest level will be for those languages which I have spend most of my time studying. This is not an indication of being an expert in the language but that my highest levels of interest and commitment lie here.
